Pizza Neapoletana

Description

Neapolitan pizza, or pizza Napoletana, is a type of pizza that originated in Naples, Italy. This style of pizza is prepared with simple and fresh ingredients: a basic dough, raw tomatoes, fresh mozzarella cheese, fresh basil, and olive oil. No fancy toppings are allowed!

One of its defining characteristics is that there is often more sauce than cheese. This leaves the middle of the pie wet or soggy and not conducive to being served by the slice. Because of this, Neapolitan pizzas are generally pretty small (about 10 to 12 inches), making them closer to the size of a personal pizza.

Ingredients

Dough:

  • Flour type 00: 292 grams
  • Water: 190 grams
  • Salt: 9 grams
  • Olive Oil: 9 grams
  • Fresh yeast: 0.54 grams

Topings:

  • Canned tomatoes
  • Fresh mozarella cheese
  • Prosciutto Cotto ham
  • Fresh basil leaves

Steps:

  1. Pour cold water into the bowl
  2. Add fresh yeast and melt it
  3. Then add about 40% of flour and mix it
  4. Leave it for 10 minutes and then add all salt
  5. Add rest of flour and all of olive oil
  6. Knead the dough for about 15 minutes until all ingredients are combined and the dough is smooth
  7. Put the dough aside for 4 hours
  8. After 4 hours divide the dough into 2 portions and set aside for another 4 hours
  9. An hour before baking, put the pizza stone in the oven and heat it at maximum temperature
  10. Knead the dough to a size of 30 cm, add the ingredients and put in the oven for 5-8 minutes until the dough is golden brown.
